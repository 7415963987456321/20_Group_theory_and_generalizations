Let $G$ be a \PMlinkescapetext{multiplicative} ordered group and $F$ its subgroup. \,We call this subgroup \PMlinkescapetext{{\em isolated}} if every element $f$ of $F$ and every element $g$ of $G$ satisfy
$$f \leqq g \leqq 1 \,\,\,\Rightarrow \,\, g\in F.$$

If an ordered group $G$ has only a finite number of isolated subgroups, then the number of proper ($\neq G$) isolated subgroups of $G$ is the \PMlinkescapetext{{\em rank}} of $G$.

\begin{thmplain}
 \, Let $G$ be an abelian ordered group with \PMlinkname{order}{OrderGroup} at least 2. \,The \PMlinkescapetext{rank} of $G$ equals one iff there is an order-preserving isomorphism from $G$ onto some subgroup of the multiplicative group of real numbers.
\end{thmplain}
