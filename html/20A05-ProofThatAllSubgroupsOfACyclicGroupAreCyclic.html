proof that all subgroups of a cyclic group are cyclic


The following is a proof that all subgroups of a cyclic group are cyclic.

Proof.

Let G be a cyclic group and H≤G. If G is trivial, then H=G, and H is cyclic. If H is the trivial subgroup, then H={eG}=⟨eG⟩, and H is cyclic. Thus, for the of the proof, it will be assumed that both G and H are nontrivial.

Let g be a generator of G. Let n be the smallest positive integer such that gn∈H.

Claim: H=⟨gn⟩

Let a∈⟨gn⟩. Then there exists z∈ℤ with a=(gn)z. Since gn∈H, we have that (gn)z∈H. Thus, a∈H. Hence, ⟨gn⟩⊆H.

Let h∈H. Then h∈G. Let x∈ℤ with h=gx. By the division algorithm, there exist q,r∈ℤ with 0≤r<n such that x=q⁢n+r. Thus, h=gx=gq⁢n+r=gq⁢n⁢gr=(gn)q⁢gr. Therefore, gr=h⁢(gn)-q. Recall that h,gn∈H. Hence, gr∈H. By choice of n, r cannot be positive. Thus, r=0. Therefore, h=(gn)q⁢g0=(gn)q⁢eG=(gn)q∈⟨gn⟩. Hence, H⊆⟨gn⟩.

This proves the claim. It follows that every subgroup of G is cyclic. ∎
