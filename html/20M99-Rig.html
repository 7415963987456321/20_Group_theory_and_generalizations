rig


A rig (R,+,⋅) is a set R together with two binary operations +:R2→R:(a,b)↦a+b and ⋅:R2→R:(a,b)↦ab, such that both (R,+) and (R,⋅) are monoids, where ⋅ distributes over +. That is if {a,b,c,d}⊂R then (a+b)⁢(c+d)=a⁢c+a⁢d+b⁢c+b⁢d. The natural numbers with ordinary addition and multiplication (𝐍,+,⋅) is a rig.

A rig (R,+,⋅) is a ring if (R,+) is a group. The integers with ordinary addition and multiplication (𝐙,+,⋅) is a ring.
