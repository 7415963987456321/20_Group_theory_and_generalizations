class equation


The conjugacy classes of a group form a partition of its elements. In a finite group, this means that the order of the group is the sum of the number of elements of the distinct conjugacy classes. For an element g of group G, we denote the centralizer in G of g by CG⁢(g). The number of elements in the conjugacy class of g is [G:CG⁢(g)], the index of CG⁢(g) in G. For an element g of the center Z⁢(G) of G, the conjugacy class of g consists of the singleton {g}. Putting this together gives us the class equation

|G|=|Z(G)|+∑i=1m[G:CG(xi)]

where the xi are elements of the distinct conjugacy classes contained in G∖Z⁢(G).
