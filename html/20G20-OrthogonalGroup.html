orthogonal group


Let Q be a non-degenerate symmetric bilinear form over the real vector space ℝn. A linear transformation T:V→V is said to preserve Q if Q⁢(T⁢x,T⁢y)=Q⁢(x,y) for all vectors x,y∈V. The subgroup of the general linear group GL⁡(V) consisting of all linear transformations that preserve Q is called the orthogonal group with respect to Q, and denoted O⁡(n,Q).

If Q is also positive definite (i.e., Q is an inner product), then O⁡(n,Q) is equivalent to the group of invertible linear transformations that preserve the standard inner product on ℝn, and in this case the group O⁡(n,Q) is usually denoted O⁡(n).

Elements of O⁡(n) are called orthogonal transformations. One can show that a linear transformation T is an orthogonal transformation if and only if T-1=TT (i.e., the inverse of T equals the transpose of T).
