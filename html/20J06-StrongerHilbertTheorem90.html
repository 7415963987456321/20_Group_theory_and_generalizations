stronger Hilbert theorem 90


Let K be a field and let K¯ be an algebraic closure of K. By K¯+ we denote the abelian group (K¯,+) and similarly K¯∗=(K¯,∗) (here the operation is multiplication). Also we let

GK¯/K=Gal⁡(K¯/K)

be the absolute Galois group of K.

Theorem 1 (Hilbert 90).

Let K be a field.

  1. 1.
    H1⁢(GK¯/K,K¯+)=0
  2. 2.
    H1⁢(GK¯/K,K¯∗)=0
  3. 3.

    If char⁡(K), the characteristic of K, does not divide m (or char⁡(K)=0) then

    H1⁢(GK¯/K,μm)≅K∗/K∗m

    where μm denotes the set of all mt⁢h-roots of unity.
