Wagner congruence


Let ρ~X⊆(X∪X-1)+ be the binary relation on the free semigroup with involution (X∪X-1)+ defined by

ρ~X={(w⁢w-1⁢w,w),(w⁢w-1⁢v⁢v-1,v⁢v-1⁢w⁢w-1)|v,w∈(X∪X-1)+}.

The Wagner congruence on X is the congruence ρX generated by ρ~X, i.e. ρX=(ρ~X)c.

A well known result of inverse semigroups theory says that the quotient

FIS⁢(X)=(X∪X-1)+/ρX

is an inverse semigroup. Moreover FIS⁢(X) is the Free Inverse Semigroup on X, in the sense that it resolve the following universal mapping problem: given an inverse semigroup S and a map Φ:X→S, a unique inverse semigroups homomorphism Φ¯:FIS⁢(X)→S exists such that the following diagram commutes:

\xymatrix⁢&⁢X⁢\ar⁢[r]ι⁢\ar⁢[d]Φ⁢&⁢FIS⁢(X)⁢\ar⁢[d⁢l]Φ¯⁢&⁢S⁢&

where ι:X→FIS⁢(X) is the projection to the quotient, i.e. ι⁢(x)=[x]ρX. It is well known from universal algebra that FIS⁢(X) is unique up to isomorphisms.

In analogous way, using the free monoid with involution (X∪X-1)∗ instead of the free semigroup with involution (X∪X-1)+, we obtain the inverse monoid

FIM⁢(X)=(X∪X-1)∗/ρX,

that is the Free Inverse Monoid on X.
