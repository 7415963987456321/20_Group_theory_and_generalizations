induced representation


Let G be a group, H⊂G a subgroup, and V a representation of H, considered as a ℤ⁢[H]–module. The induced representation of ρ on G, denoted IndHG⁡(V), is the ℤ⁢[G]–module whose underlying vector space is the direct sum

⊕σ∈G/Hσ⁢V

of formal translates of V by left cosets σ in G/H, and whose multiplication operation is defined by choosing a set {gσ}σ∈G/H of coset representatives and setting

g⁢(σ⁢v):=τ⁢(h⁢v)

where τ is the unique left coset of G/H containing g⋅gσ (i.e., such that g⋅gσ=gτ⋅h for some h∈H).

One easily verifies that the representation IndHG⁡(V) is independent of the choice of coset representatives {gσ}.
