3 History

The name central product appears to have been coined by Philip Hall [1, Section 3.2] though the principal concept of such a product appears in earlier work (e.g. [2, Theorem II]). Hall describes central products as ‚Äú‚Ä¶the group obtained from the direct product by identifying the centres of the direct factors‚Ä¶‚Äù. The modern definition clearly out grows this original version as now centers may be only partially identified.
