conjugacy class


Two elements g and g′ of a group G are said to be conjugate if there exists h∈G such that g′=h⁢g⁢h-1. Conjugacy of elements is an equivalence relation, and the equivalence classes of G are called conjugacy classes.

Two subsets S and T of G are said to be conjugate if there exists g∈G such that

T={g⁢s⁢g-1∣s∈S}⊂G.

In this situation, it is common to write g⁢S⁢g-1 for T to denote the fact that everything in T has the form g⁢s⁢g-1 for some s∈S. We say that two subgroups of G are conjugate if they are conjugate as subsets.
