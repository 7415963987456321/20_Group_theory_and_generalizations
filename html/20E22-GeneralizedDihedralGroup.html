generalized dihedral group


Let A be an abelian group. The generalized dihedral group Dih⁡(A) is the semidirect product A⋊C2, where C2 is the cyclic group of order 2, and the generator (http://planetmath.org/Generator) of C2 maps elements of A to their inverses.

If A is cyclic, then Dih⁡(A) is called a dihedral group. The finite dihedral group Dih⁡(Cn) is commonly denoted by Dn or D2⁢n (the differing conventions being a source of confusion). The infinite dihedral group Dih⁡(C∞) is denoted by D∞, and is isomorphic to the free product C2*C2 of two cyclic groups of order 2.

If A is an elementary abelian 2-group, then so is Dih⁡(A). If A is not an elementary abelian 2-group, then Dih⁡(A) is non-abelian.

The subgroup A×{1} of Dih⁡(A) is of index 2, and every element of Dih⁡(A) that is not in this subgroup has order 2. This property in fact characterizes generalized dihedral groups, in the sense that if a group G has a subgroup N of index 2 such that all elements of the complement G∖N are of order 2, then N is abelian and G≅Dih⁡(N).
