symmetric group is generated by adjacent transpositions


Theorem 1.

The symmetric group on {1,2,…,n} is generated by the permutations

(1,2),(2,3),…,(n-1,n).
Proof.

We proceed by induction on n. If n=2, the theorem is trivially true because the the group only consists of the identity and a single transposition.

Suppose, then, that we know permutations of n numbers are generated by transpositions of successive numbers. Let ϕ be a permutation of {1,2,…,n+1}. If ϕ⁢(n+1)=n+1, then the restriction of ϕ to {1,2,…,n} is a permutation of n numbers, hence, by hypothesis, it can be expressed as a product of transpositions.

Suppose that, in addition, ϕ⁢(n+1)=m with m≠n+1. Consider the following product of transpositions:

(n⁢n+1)⁢(n-1⁢n)⁢⋯⁢(m+1⁢m+1)⁢(m⁢m+1)

It is easy to see that acting upon m with this product of transpositions produces +1. Therefore, acting upon n+1 with the permutation

(n⁢n+1)⁢(n-1⁢n)⁢⋯⁢(m+1⁢m+1)⁢(m⁢m+1)⁢ϕ

produces n+1. Hence, the restriction of this permutation to {1,2,…,n} is a permutation of n numbers, so, by hypothesis, it can be expressed as a product of transpositions. Since a transposition is its own inverse, it follows that ϕ may also be expressed as a product of transpositions. ∎
