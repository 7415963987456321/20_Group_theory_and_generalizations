Zeta function of a group


Let G be a finitely generated group and let 𝒳 be a family of finite index subgroups of G. Define

an(𝒳)=|{H∈𝒳∣|G:H|=n}|.

Note that these numbers are finite since a finitely generated group has only finitely many subgroups of a given index. We define the zeta function of the family 𝒳 to be the formal Dirichlet series

ζ𝒳⁢(s)=∑n=1∞an⁢(𝒳)⁢n-s.

Two important special cases are the zeta function counting all subgroups and the zeta function counting normal subgroups. Let 𝒮⁢(G) and 𝒩⁢(G) be the families of all finite index subgroups of G and of all finite index normal subgroups of G, respectively. We write an⩽⁢(G)=an⁢(𝒮⁢(G)) and an⊴⁢(G)=an⁢(𝒩⁢(G)) and define

ζG⩽(s)=ζ𝒮⁢(G)(s)=∑H⩽fG|G:H|-s,

and

ζG⊴(s)=ζ𝒩⁢(G)(s)=∑N⊴fG|G:N|-s.

If, in addition, G is nilpotent, then ζG⩽ has a decomposition as a formal Euler product

ζG⩽⁢(s)=∏p⁢ primeζG,p⩽⁢(s),

where

ζG,p⩽⁢(s)=∑i=0∞api⩽⁢(G)⁢p-i⁢s.

An analogous result holds for the normal zeta function ζG⊴. The result for both ζG⩽ and ζG⊴ can be proved using properties of the profinite completion of G. However, a simpler proof for the normal zeta function is provided by the fact that a finite nilpotent group decomposes into a direct product of its Sylow subgroups. These results allow the zeta functions to be expressed in terms of p-adic integrals, which can in turn be used to prove (using some high-powered machinery) that ζG,p⩽⁢(s) and ζG,p⊴⁢(s) are rational functions in p and p-s.

In the case when G is a 𝒯-group, that is, G is finitely generated, torsion free, and nilpotent, define αG⩽ to be the abscissa of convergence of ζG⩽. That is, αG⩽ is the smallest α∈ℝ such that ζG⩽ defines a holomorphic function in the right half-plane {z∈ℂ⁢∣ℜ⁡(z)>⁢α}. It can then be shown that αG⩽⩽h⁢(G), where h⁢(G) is the Hirsch number of G. Therefore, if G is a 𝒯-group, ζG⩽ defines a holomorphic function in some right half-plane.
