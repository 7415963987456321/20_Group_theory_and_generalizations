McAlister covering theorem


A subset X in an inverse semigroup S is called unitary if for any elements x∈X and s∈S, x⁢s∈X or s⁢x∈X implies s∈X.

An inverse semigroup is E-unitary if its semigroup of idempotents is unitary.

Theorem.

Let S be an inverse semigroup; then, there exists an E-unitary inverse semigroup P and a surjective, idempotent-separating homomorphism θ:P→S.

Also, if S is finite, then P may be chosen to be finite as well.

Note that a homomorphism is idempotent-separating if it is injective on idempotents.
