Frobenius reciprocity


Let V be a finite-dimensional representation of a finite group G, and let W be a representation of a subgroup H⊂G. Then the characters of V and W satisfy the inner product relation

(χInd⁡(W),χV)=(χW,χRes⁡(V))

where Ind and Res denote the induced representation IndHG and the restriction representation ResHG.

The Frobenius reciprocity theorem is often given in the stronger form which states that Res and Ind are adjoint functors between the category of G–modules and the category of H–modules:

HomH⁡(W,Res⁡(V))=HomG⁡(Ind⁡(W),V),

or, equivalently

V⊗Ind⁡(W)=Ind⁡(Res⁡(V)⊗W).
