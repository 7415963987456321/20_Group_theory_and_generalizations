free products and group actions


Theorem 1.

(See Lang, Exercise 54 p. 81) Suppose G1,…⁢Gn are subgroups of G that generate G. Suppose further that G acts on a set S and that there are subsets S1,S2,…⁢Sn⊂S, and some s∈S-∪Si such that for each 1≤i≤n, the following holds for each g∈Gi,g≠e:

  • •

    g⁢(Sj)⊂Si if j≠i, and

  • •

    g⁢(s)∈Si.

Then G=G1⋆…⋆Gn (where ⋆ denotes the free product).

Proof: Any g∈G can be written g=g1⁢g2⁢…⁢gk with gi∈Gji,ji≠ji+1,gi≠e, since the Gi generate G. Thus there is a surjective homomorphism ϕ:∐Gi↠G (since ∐Gi, as the coproduct, has this universal property). We must show ker⁡ϕ is trivial. Choose g1⁢g2⁢…⁢gk as above. Then gk⁢(s)∈Sjk, gk-1⁢(gk⁢(s))∈Sjk-1, and so forth, so that g1(g2(…(gk(s)…)∈Sj1. But e⁢(s)=s∉Sj1. Thus ϕ⁢(g1⁢g2⁢…⁢gk)≠e, and ϕ is injective.
