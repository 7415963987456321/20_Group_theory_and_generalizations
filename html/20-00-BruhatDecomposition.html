Bruhat decomposition


Bruhat decomposition is the name for the fact that B\G/B=W, where G is a reductive group, B a Borel subgroup, and W the Weyl group. Less canonically, one can write G=B⁢W⁢B.

In the case of the general linear group G=G⁢Ln, B is the group of nonsingular upper triangular matrices, and W is the collection of permutation matrices (and is isomorphic to Sn). Any nonsingular matrix can thus be written uniquely as a product of an upper triangular matrix, a permutation matrix, and another upper triangular matrix.
