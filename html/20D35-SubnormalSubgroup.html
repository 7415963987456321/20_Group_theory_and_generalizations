subnormal subgroup


Let G be a group, and H a subgroup of G. Then H is a subnormal subgroup of G if there is a natural number n and subgroups H0,…,Hn of G such that

H=H0◁H1◁⋯◁Hn=G,

where Hi is a normal subgroup of Hi+1 for i=0,…,n-1.

Subnormality is a , as normality of subgroups is not transitive.

We may write H⁢sn⁡G or H◁◁G or H⁢⊴⁢⊴⁢G to indicate that H is a subnormal subgroup of G.

In a nilpotent group, all subgroups are subnormal.

Subnormal subgroups are ascendant and descendant.
