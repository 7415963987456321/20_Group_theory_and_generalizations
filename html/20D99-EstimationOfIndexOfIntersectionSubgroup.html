estimation of index of intersection subgroup


Theorem.  If H1,H2,…,Hn are subgroups of G, then

[G:⋂i=1nHi]≦∏i=1n[G:Hi].

Proof.  We prove here only the case  n=2;  the general case may be handled by the induction.

Let  H1∩H2:=K.  Let R be the set of the right cosets of K and Ri the set of the right cosets of Hi  (i=1, 2).  Define the relation ϱ from R to R1×R2 as

ϱ:={(Kx,(H1x,H2x))⋮x∈G}.

We then have the equivalent (http://planetmath.org/Equivalent3) conditions

K⁢x=K⁢y,
x⁢y-1∈K,
x⁢y-1∈H1 ∧ x⁢y-1∈H2,
H1⁢x=H1⁢y ∧ H2⁢x=H2⁢y,
(H1⁢x,H2⁢x)=(H1⁢y,H2⁢y),

whence ϱ is a mapping and injective,  ϱ:R→R1×R2.  i.e. it is a bijection from R onto the subset  {ϱ⁢(x)⁢⋮⁢x∈R}  of R1×R2.  Therefore,

card⁡(R)≦card⁡(R1×R2)=card⁡(R1)⋅card⁡(R2).

As a consequence one obtains the

Theorem (Poincaré).  The index of the intersection of finitely many subgroups with finite indices (http://planetmath.org/Coset) is finite.
