natural projection


Proposition.  If H is a normal subgroup of a group G, then the mapping

φ:G→G/H where φ⁢(x)=x⁢H⁢∀x∈G

is a surjective homomorphism whose kernel is H.

Proof.  Because every coset appears as image, the mapping φ is surjective.  It is also homomorphic, since for all elements x,y of G, one has

φ⁢(x⁢y)=(x⁢y)⁢H=x⁢H⋅y⁢H=φ⁢(x)⁢φ⁢(y).

The identity element of the factor group G/H is the coset  e⁢H=H,  whence

ker(φ)={x∈G⋮φ(x)=H}={x∈G⋮xH=H}=H.

The mapping φ in the proposition is called natural projection or canonical homomorphism.
