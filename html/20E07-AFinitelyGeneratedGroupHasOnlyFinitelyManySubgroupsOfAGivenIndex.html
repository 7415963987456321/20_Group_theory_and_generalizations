A finitely generated group has only finitely many subgroups of a given index


Let G be a finitely generated group and let n be a positive integer. Let H be a subgroup of G of index n and consider the action of G on the coset space (G:H) by right multiplication. Label the cosets 1,…,n, with the coset H labelled by 1. This gives a homomorphism ϕ:G→Sn. Now, x∈H if and only if H⁢x=H, that is, G fixes the coset H. Therefore, H=StabG⁢(1)={g∈G∣1⁢(g⁢ϕ)=1}, and this is completely determined by ϕ. Now let X be a finite generating set for G. Then ϕ is determined by the images x⁢ϕ of the generators x∈X. There are |Sn|=n! choices for the image of each x∈X, so there are at most n!|X| homomorphisms G→Sn. Hence, there are only finitely many possibilities for H.
